# PRD-504: Channel Partner and Revenue Share

Status: Draft

## Purpose

Define product requirements for counselors, schools, agencies, and institutions acting as trusted distribution channels for the full Decision Workspace.

## Channel Roles

### Counselor Channel

A counselor invites a student or family into the platform and may continue providing guided service inside the workspace.

### Institution Channel

A school, agency, or organization distributes the platform to students, families, or affiliated counselors. The institution may receive channel-level attribution and revenue share when eligible.

### Nested Channel

An institution and a counselor may both participate in the same conversion. For example, a school may distribute the platform broadly while a specific counselor supports a student's workspace. Both may be eligible for revenue share under defined attribution rules.

### Self-guided Channel

A student or parent may enter without an attached counselor or institution. This path should remain available, but it is not the primary early distribution thesis.

## Counselor Incentives

Counselor distribution depends on three incentives:

- **Earn**: the counselor receives revenue share, credits, or other upside from eligible student/parent conversions.
- **Save**: the platform reduces time spent on intake, research, data gathering, explanation, reporting, and communication.
- **Sell**: the platform improves the counselor's professional credibility, client conversion, and renewal through clearer data-backed service delivery.

## Institution Incentives

Institutions may participate as direct purchasers, channel partners, or both.

Institution distribution becomes attractive when the platform helps the institution:

- provide a common decision workspace to many students;
- support or standardize affiliated counselor workflows;
- improve the perceived quality of advising services;
- receive channel revenue share from student or parent conversions;
- offer a credible data-backed service without absorbing all usage costs upfront.

## Revenue Share Principles

- Revenue share should reward the trusted channel that contributed to the paid conversion.
- Nested institution and counselor participation should be possible when both contributed to the conversion.
- Revenue share eligibility should be understandable to channel partners before they distribute the platform.
- Refunds, cancellations, disputes, and chargebacks should have clear product-level consequences for revenue share.
- User trust should be protected. Revenue share should not make the platform or channel partner appear to compromise decision quality.

## Channel Safety Principles

- The platform should strengthen the channel's service relationship rather than replace it.
- The channel should be visible as the trusted inviter or service partner when appropriate.
- The platform should not position itself as a substitute counselor in counselor-guided workspaces.
- Channel partners should retain a clear role in explaining, reviewing, or acting on workspace outputs when they are attached to the user.
- Self-guided users should still receive complete product value without requiring a counselor.

## Open Questions

- What revenue share models are acceptable for counselors, institutions, and nested channels?
- Should users be explicitly told that the inviting channel may receive revenue share?
- How should attribution work when a student has multiple invitations?
- Can an institution set the workspace as required without subsidizing student payment?
