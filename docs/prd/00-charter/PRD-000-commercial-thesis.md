# PRD-000: Commercial Thesis

Status: Draft

## Core Question

Who pays, for what outcome, why now, and why repeatedly?

## Primary Commercial Thesis

The platform's primary cash flow comes from becoming the required Decision Workspace for counselor-led and institution-led admissions services.

Counselors, schools, agencies, and institutions act as trusted distribution channels. They introduce students and parents into a branded decision workspace. Students or parents pay to unlock the full Decision Workspace, then continue either self-guided or with counselor/institution support.

## Core Paid Product

The full Decision Workspace is the core paid product.

It is not merely a search tool, report generator, or counselor service permission. It is the paid environment where students and parents can make school-major decisions using trusted data, explainable analysis, comparisons, reports, next steps, and optional collaboration.

## Distribution Model

### Counselor-led Distribution

A counselor invites a student or family into a workspace and uses the platform to improve intake, research, explanation, reporting, and collaboration.

### Institution-led Distribution

A school, agency, or organization distributes the workspace to students, families, or affiliated counselors. The institution may act as a direct purchaser, a channel partner, or both.

### Nested Distribution

An institution and counselor may both contribute to the same paid conversion. The product should support channel attribution and revenue share rules for nested distribution when eligible.

### Self-guided Fallback

Students and parents may also use the workspace without an attached counselor. In this mode, the user acts as their own counselor and pays to unlock the same decision workspace for self-guided progress.

## Required Workspace Thesis

The strongest distribution occurs when a counselor or institution makes the platform part of its standard service workflow:

```text
Counselor / institution adopts platform as workspace
  -> Student enters workspace to receive service
    -> Student or parent pays to unlock complete decision work
      -> Student continues self-guided or with attached support
        -> Channel partner receives attribution and revenue share when eligible
```

The product should help channels move through three levels of distribution strength:

1. **Referral Channel**: The channel recommends the platform, but usage is optional.
2. **Preferred Workspace**: The channel defaults to the platform for service delivery, with exceptions allowed.
3. **Required Workspace**: The channel makes the platform the formal entry point for intake, analysis, collaboration, reporting, or payment.

## Counselor Incentive Thesis

Counselors will distribute the platform when it helps them earn, save, and sell:

- **Earn**: receive referral revenue share, credits, or other financial upside from eligible conversions.
- **Save**: reduce time spent on research, data gathering, explanation, report preparation, and family communication.
- **Sell**: improve professional credibility, client conversion, and renewal by delivering clearer, more trusted decision work.

## Institution Incentive Thesis

Institutions will distribute the platform when it helps them standardize service quality, create scalable student access, support affiliated counselors, and participate in revenue share from student or parent conversions.

## Channel Safety Principle

The platform must strengthen channel partners rather than disintermediate them.

Counselors and institutions should feel that the platform protects their client relationship, supports their brand or role, and gives them better service infrastructure. It should not make them feel replaced by the platform.

## Success Criteria

- A student or parent understands that payment unlocks a complete Decision Workspace, not merely a generic subscription.
- A counselor can invite a student without feeling that the platform will take over the client relationship.
- An institution can distribute the platform as a standard workflow without needing to buy every seat upfront.
- Revenue share can be explained as channel participation in a trusted service workflow.
- Self-guided users can continue without a counselor while preserving the same workspace value proposition.
