# PRD Glossary

## Core Terms

- **Data Asset**: A product-recognized body of admissions, institution, ranking, academic, or outcomes data with defined authority, freshness, limitations, and downstream consumers.
- **Data Asset Contract**: The PRD section that defines what a data asset means to the product and how it may be used.
- **Source Authority**: The organization or publication treated as the origin of a data asset.
- **Verification Cue**: A user-visible or internal traceability marker that ties a displayed claim to its source and version.
- **Freshness Requirement**: The product expectation for how often a source must be reviewed, refreshed, or marked stale.
- **Downstream Consumer**: A PRD or product surface that depends on a data asset or intelligence product.
- **Decision Product**: A product capability that combines data assets into an explanation, score, comparison, recommendation, or warning.
- **Decision Profile**: The user's evolving college-major fit profile, combining goals, constraints, preferences, and data-backed signals.
- **Sponsored Access**: A commercial mechanism where a counselor or organization grants a student access to paid capabilities under defined boundaries.

## Data Families

- **Institution Data**: School identity, public/private control, geography, size, tuition, admissions attributes, and other institution-level facts.
- **Ranking Data**: Published college or program rankings from named sources and years, including ties and category definitions.
- **Academic Program Data**: University-specific programs, schools, majors, curriculum requirements, prerequisites, and CIP-aligned mappings.
- **Outcomes Data**: Salary, employment, ROI, labor market, and student outcome signals.

## PRD Status Values

- **Draft**: Product intent is being shaped and should not be used as implementation-ready source.
- **Review**: Product scope is stable enough for stakeholder review.
- **Approved**: Product requirements are accepted as source of truth for planning.
- **Superseded**: A newer PRD replaces this document; keep it for history only.
